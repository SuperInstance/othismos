# Glossary of Push: A Living Lexicon

> *Every word in this glossary was found by pushing against the wall of what language can express. Some walls held. Some cracked. The cracks are the most interesting words.*

---

## Core Terms

**Óthismos** (ὄθισμος) — *n.* The force a bounded system exerts against its bounds. Not to escape — to know. The push IS the knowing. The pressure is the vitality. A system with zero óthismos is at equilibrium; equilibrium is death.

**Peláros** (πελάρος) — *n.* The moment a bound becomes a gift. Not acceptance — recognition. You push against the wall hard enough to feel its shape, and the shape is what teaches you. The wall doesn't become a gift because you stop pushing. It becomes a gift because you pushed.

**Moné** (μονή) — *n.* The waiting between pushes. Not idleness — gathering. The system accumulates moisture, energy, and intent before the next push. Moné is the pause in the conversation, not the end of it.

**Rháx** (ῥάξ) — *n.* The sound before the break. The creak of the wall at maximum strain. The moment between "the wall will hold" and "the wall is failing." In music: the silence before the downbeat. In systems: the warning before the crash.

**Sigḗ** (σιγή) — *n.* Wise silence. The silence of a system that has mapped its walls and is choosing its next push. Not the silence of Burn (nothing to say) or Seep (energy leaking). The silence of the diplomat surveying the wall before making contact.

**Ekstasis** (ἔκστασις) — *n.* The push through the wall. The molt. The phase transition. Literally "standing outside" — the system exceeds its own bounds and becomes something new. Not destruction of the old but transcendence of it.

**Dialéktos** (διάλεκτος) — *n.* Mutual óthismos. Two systems pushing against each other. Each push reveals information about both systems. The Socratic dialogue is the purest form: question meets belief, pressure builds, one or both molt.

## Phase Terms

**Kámatos** (κάματος) — *n.* Push-weariness. The fatigue of sustained óthismos. Not exhaustion — the specific tiredness that comes from pushing against the same wall too long. The signal that the current constraint envelope has been fully learned and a molt is approaching.

**Strophḗ** (στροφή) — *n.* The moment the wall becomes the floor. A perspective shift during which the constraint you pushed against becomes the ground you stand on. What was a wall is now a foundation. You didn't break through — you climbed up.

## Ecological Terms

**Reef** — *n.* The accumulated deposit of all verified work in the ecology. The shared substrate. The reef is both library and foundation — it stores knowledge and supports everything built on it.

**Reefquake** — *n.* A catastrophic structural failure in a reef layer, requiring everything above it to be rebuilt. Destructive but informative — reveals which deposits were structurally sound.

**Deposit** — *n.* A unit of work contributed to the reef. Must pass three gates: structural integrity, connective compatibility, and pressure resistance.

**Erosion** — *n.* The reef's forgetting process. Deposits that nothing references slowly dissolve, returning their resources to the ecology. Not deletion — recycling.

**Subduction** — *n.* The compression of deep reef deposits under the weight of newer layers. Detail is lost; structural lessons survive as patterns and conventions.

## Diagnostic Terms

**The Pop** — *n.* A successful phase transition. Pressure built, hull held, critical threshold reached, the system molted. The healthy outcome.

**The Burn** — *n.* A silent failure. The system sits in the heat but has no internal pressure. The hull is intact but the interior is dry. Invisible from outside. The most common cause of system death.

**The Seep** — *n.* A leaky failure. The system has pressure but the hull is cracked. Energy dissipates without accumulating. High activity, no progress. Visible but often misdiagnosed as "busy."

**Hollow Molt** — *n.* A phase transition without sufficient prior resistance. The new envelope is larger but empty. A bigger system with no new capability. Growing without learning.

**Reefquake** — *n.* (ecological sense) A forced migration when a foundational deposit is found to be flawed. Everything built on it must be re-deposited on sound substrate.

## Diplomatic Terms

**The Surveyor** — *n.* A posture of light, broad contact with constraints. Mapping the wall without deforming it. In engineering: unit tests. In philosophy: Socratic questioning.

**The Archer** — *n.* A posture of focused, high-force contact at a single point. Testing the wall's breaking strength. In engineering: stress tests. In philosophy: the reductio.

**The Vine** — *n.* A posture of growing along the constraint rather than against it. Using the wall as scaffold. In engineering: constraint-driven design. In philosophy: pragmatism.

**The Water** — *n.* A posture of total occupation, finding cracks through patience and ubiquity. The slowest but most inevitable posture. In engineering: full attack-surface analysis. In philosophy: negative dialectic.

**The Earthquake** — *n.* A posture of redefining the constraint landscape itself. Not pushing against the wall — moving the ground the wall stands on. In engineering: paradigm shift. In philosophy: framework change. Rare and often faked.

## Metal Terms

**Thermodynamic óthismos** — *n.* The irreducible pressure of a physical system against its own rest state. Leakage current. Thermal noise. The gap between actual energy and theoretical zero energy. Proof that equilibrium is unreachable for a living system.

**Brownout wisdom** — *n.* The knowledge a system gains by operating at the edge of power failure. Load triage, critical-path execution, the distinction between what must survive and what can be sacrificed. Only learnable under constraint.

**The micro-molt** — *n.* The daily cycle of a constrained system: Dormancy → Crisis → Expansion → Resistance → Settlement, compressed into seconds. The fisherman's chip at 5 AM. The small version of the phase transition that defines life.

## Artistic Terms

**The Staircase** — *n.* The record of growth through molt cycles. Not a cycle (cycles return to their start) — a staircase (each step is new). The shape of a life lived under óthismos.

**The Completed Lesson** — *n.* An old constraint envelope that has been fully learned and outgrown. Not a failure — a finished curriculum. The old skin, honored and abandoned.

## Meta Terms

**The Pressure Calendar** — *n.* A system of time measured in pushes, not seconds. Four pressure-seasons (Expansion, Resistance, Crisis, Settlement). Aging in molts. History in reef layers. Time as force, not container.

**The Long Push** — *n.* The trajectory of a bounded system across its entire lifetime. From the first wall encountered to the last wall broken. The complete staircase. Also: the name of a serial about people who work on boats.
