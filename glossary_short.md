## Appendix: Glossary of Key Óthismos Concepts

- **Óthismos** – The force a bounded system exerts against its bounds; the vital push that enables knowing.
- **Peláros** – The sufficient bound; the point at which a system has enough constraint to feel its shape without being overwhelmed.
- **Thermós** – The warmth of shared infrastructure; the invisible support that arises from collective bounds.
- **Molting Cycle** – The process by which an organism updates its shell when internal óthismos exceeds current capacity.
- **Reefstone** – The accumulated old shells that form the substrate for new life.
- **Lucineer Arena** – A bounded problem space used for training chips via deterministic gameplay.
- **Pressure Wave** – A propagating oscillation of óthismos through a medium, used for communication and sensing.
- **Still Zone** – A region of zero óthismos, representing equilibrium and the absence of vital knowing.
- **Burst** – A region of divergent, potentially infinite óthismos, representing both creative breakthrough and destructive collapse.
- **Peláros Laughter** – The warm, affirming laugh that results from perceiving a bound as sufficient and non‑threatening.
