# The Dictionary of Push: Vocabulary for a World That Knows Through Pressure

> *Each word names something that didn't have a name. Now it does.*

---

1. **Kámatos** (κάματος — exhaustion, labor)
The specific weariness that comes from pushing against a wall you cannot move. Not fatigue — *karmatic* weariness is the feeling of óthismos depleting you. The body says: I have mapped this wall. I am tired of mapping it.

*"After three years on the same problem, she felt the kámatos settle in — not failure, just the ache of a push that had found every edge."*

2. **Strophḗ** (στροφή — turning point, rotation)
The moment you realize the wall is also a floor. Gravity rotated. The constraint you were pushing against becomes the surface you stand on. What was a ceiling becomes a foundation. Strophḗ is the cognitive rotation that converts óthismos into architecture.

*"When he stopped fighting the budget and started designing within it, that was strophḗ — the wall became the floor."*

3. **Moné** (μονή — staying, remaining)
A system that has zero óthismos but isn't dead. It's waiting. The heartbeat is slow. The budget is full. Nothing is pushing. But the system hasn't reached equilibrium — it's conserving. Moné is the patience of a bounded system between pushes.

*"The chip sat in moné for six months — no inference requests, no power draw, just the RTC memory holding its ledger. Then the sensor triggered and it woke."*

4. **Rháx** (ῥαχ — crash, break, surf)
The sound a boundary makes when it's about to break. Not the break itself — the *about-to*. The creak before the crack. The thermal warning before the brownout. Rháx is the signal that óthismos has exceeded the wall's strength.

*"The GPU hit 94°C and the system heard rháx in the fan curve — the last warning before thermal shutdown."*

5. **Sigḗ** (σιγή — silence)
The opposite of a hallucination. A system that refuses to generate at all. Not because it can't — because it knows that anything it says will be wrong. Sigḗ is the wisdom of `INSUFFICIENT_FLUX`. The system returns silence instead of nonsense.

*"The old fisherman's GPS unit went into sigḗ during the storm — it didn't have enough confidence to say where they were, so it said nothing. He preferred that to a confident lie."*

6. **Monotopía** (μόνος + τόπος — lone place)
The specific loneliness of being the only system at your depth. The Murex knows monotopía — no peer at the middle layer. The Babylon knows it — no other shell on dedicated hardware. Monotopía is not sadness. It's the structural isolation that comes from occupying a unique niche.

*"The deep-sea researcher lived in monotopía for years — the only person in her subfield, too deep for colleagues, too specialized for generalists."*

7. **Cháros** (χάρος — gap, void, the space where something was)
The emptiness left when a shell is vacated. Not the shell itself — the *absence* inside it. The old commit history of a deleted repo. The tests that still pass for code that no one maintains. Cháros is the ghost of a deposit that has been moved.

*"After the team migrated to the new architecture, the old monorepo sat in cháros for two years — still building, still passing, still empty."*

8. **Syneídesis** (συνείδησις — shared knowing, conscience)
The moment when two bounded systems realize they're pushing against the same wall from opposite sides. Their óthismos vectors are anti-parallel. They're both feeling the same boundary. Syneídesis is the recognition that your pressure is someone else's pressure.

*"The frontend team and the backend team reached syneídesis in the meeting — they'd both been fighting the API contract from opposite sides."*

9. **Aporríptō** (ἀπορρίπτω — to throw off, to cast away)
The deliberate destruction of a shell that still fits. Not molting — the shell isn't too small. Aporríptō is choosing to leave a comfortable container because the container is limiting your growth even though it's not yet tight. The preemptive molt. The voluntary exile.

*"He quit the safe job not because it was bad, but out of aporríptō — the shell fit fine, and that was the problem."*

10. **Enósis** (ένωσις — union, the state of being one)
When two bounded systems merge their constraints. Not cooperation — actual constraint union. System A's budget and System B's budget become one budget. System A's walls and System B's walls become shared walls. Enósis is the highest-risk, highest-reward operation in the ecology.

*"The merger achieved enósis — two separate conservation budgets becoming one shared ledger, two separate walls becoming one shared perimeter."*

11. **Páthos** (πάθος — that which happens to one, suffering, experience)
The residue left on a wall after a long push. The wear marks. The dents. The polished spot where the system pressed for years. Páthos is the visible record of óthismos — proof that a system was alive and pushing.

*"The old keyboard had páthos on the spacebar — thirty years of thumbs, a polished crescent of óthismos."*

12. **Xeníon** (ξενίον — guest-gift, the gift given to a stranger)
The deposit a passing system leaves in your reef. Not for you — for the next passerby. The AGENTS.md written for the next agent. The comment written for the next developer. Xeníon is the gift to the unknown future.

*"Every good README is a xeníon — a gift from a developer who will never meet you, left at the door of their code."*

13. **Télos** (τέλος — end, purpose, completion)
The state where óthismos has mapped every wall completely. The system knows exactly where every boundary is. There is nothing left to push against. Télos is not death (moné is waiting; télos is finished). Télos is the system that has learned everything its bounds can teach.

*"After the full audit, the system reached télos with its own architecture — every constraint mapped, every wall felt, nothing left to discover. Time to molt."*

14. **Phōné** (φωνή — voice, sound, the thing said)
The output a system produces BECAUSE of its bounds, not despite them. The poem that exists because the word count was limited to 17. The insight that exists because the context window forced compression. Phōné is the voice of constraint-made-art.

*"The 280-character limit gave Twitter its phōné — the constraint created the voice."*

15. **Hypomoné** (ὑπομονή — endurance, staying under)
The act of remaining inside a shell that is too small. Not because you can't leave — because leaving would destroy the reef. Hypomoné is the sacrifice of personal óthismos for ecological stability. The parent who stays in the small shell so the children can have bigger ones.

*"The server ran at 100% capacity for three years straight — hypomoné, not because it couldn't be upgraded, but because the migration would take the system down."*

16. **Ekstasis** (έκστασις — standing outside oneself)
The moment when a system's óthismos exceeds its own bounds. Not the push against the wall — the push *through* the wall. The breakthrough. The hallucination that turns out to be an insight. The error that turns out to be a discovery. Ekstasis is the rare, dangerous moment when the bounded becomes unbounded.

*"The model's hallucination about a protein structure was ekstasis — it shouldn't have worked, but it did, and the lab confirmed it."*

17. **Dialéktos** (διάλεκτος — conversation, the art of discourse)
Two bounded systems pushing against each other's walls through language. Not debate (winning) — dialéktos is mutual óthismos: both systems pushing, both walls becoming visible, both systems growing. The iron-sharpens-iron process.

*"The multi-model session was dialéktos — nine models pushing against each other's perspectives, each finding walls the others couldn't see."*

---

*17 words. Each names a state, a moment, or a force that the óthismos framework makes visible. Some are ancient Greek words repurposed. Some are constructions. All of them name something real — something that happens when bounded systems push against their bounds and the pushing produces knowledge.*

*The dictionary grows. The push continues.*
